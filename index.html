"Apo Whang-Od"

Biography of Apo Whang-Od

Life:
Apo Whang-Od was born in February 17, 1917 in Buscalan, Tinglayan, Kalinga Philippines. During her teenage years she was in a relationship with a warrior named Ang-Batang but the villagers are against thier affair thus an arranged marriage was created between Whang-ods bestfriend Hogkajon and Ang-Batang. Ang-Batang died during a logging activity so Whang-Od vows not to marry anyone though she had relation with other warrior of the tribe she remained not married and did not have any children and no direct descendants on her bloodline.


Career:
Started at the young age of 15yrs Apo Wang-Od learn the ancient technique of tattoo called "Batok". She learn the art from her Father who was considered as a Master Tattooist in the region. The ink use for tattoo is compose of indigenous materials with a mixture of Charcoal and water. It is tapped into the skin using thorn from calamansi or pomelo tree. Thi way of tattoo dated back thousand years and is relatively painful. The designs is found in nature or geometric shapes.

During the Early days, Headhunters has to earn thier tattoos by protecting the village or killing an enemy the term for this tattoo is "Fi-ing" it is tattooed on males chest and arms, since headhunting was discourage by the government Fi-ing has to stop and it was last practice during 1972.

Womens of the village get tattooed for aesthetic purposes the term for this tattoo is "Fatok" this is to show the the Beauty and Wealth of the women and this depends on the design that they are getting. The womans family has to pay the tattoo artist a Piglet or bundle of rice locally known as "dalan".

During these modern days she is earning money through donations from the tourist that she is giving tattoo.


legacy:
Apo Whang-od's art is recognized and made a huge impact with FIlipino's culture and tradition it kept the tradition alive and known inside and out of the Philippines. She is now training her Grandniece along with other 20 kalingayen apprentice to continue the tradition and not die with her.

Although the Batok tattoo is still in practice it does not carry the same meaning as when they are being earn during warfare. The design is still base on nature and geometric style and each "Mambabatok" (tattoo artist) has their own signature design like the 3 dots from Apo Whang-Od.
